Budget

Here's a rough breakdown of potential costs for pilot training and living expenses for a foreign student in Canada:

Pilot Training Costs: Private Pilot License (PPL): The cost of obtaining a PPL in Canada can range from $10,000 to $15,000 or more. This includes flight hours, aircraft rental, instructor fees, ground school, written exams, and Transport Canada fees. Commercial Pilot License (CPL): The cost of CPL training can range from $30,000 to $50,000 or more. This includes additional flight hours, advanced training, written and practical exams, and fees. Multi-Engine Rating (MER): If you pursue a multi-engine rating, you might need to allocate an additional $5,000 to $10,000.

Living Expenses: Accommodation: The cost of accommodation varies based on the city or town you choose for training. On average, monthly rent for a shared apartment or student housing can range from $600 to $1,200. Food: The monthly cost of groceries and meals can range from $250 to $500. Transportation: Public transportation or car expenses, depending on your location and preferences, might cost around $100 to $300 per month. Health Insurance: As an international student, you'll likely need health insurance, which can cost around $50 to $100 per month. Other Expenses: Miscellaneous expenses like books, study materials, and personal items might add up to approximately $100 to $200 per month.

Visa and Study Permit Costs: Study Permit: The study permit application fee is currently CAD $150 for most international students. However, the requirements and fees might change, so check the latest information on the official Canadian immigration website. Visa: Depending on your country of origin, you might need a temporary resident visa (TRV) to enter Canada. The visa application fee is currently CAD $100.
